* line-fill.el

An Emacs package that splits a paragraph into one sentence per line.

** Usage

Bind ~line-fill-paragraph~ to a key, e.g.:

#+begin_src emacs-lisp
(global-set-key (kbd "M-q") #'line-fill-paragraph)
#+end_src

or e.g.

#+begin_src emacs-lisp
(define-key LaTeX-mode-map (kbd "M-q") #'line-fill-paragraph)
#+end_src

- Called normally :: splits the paragraph at point into one sentence per line.
- Called with a prefix argument (~C-u M-q~) :: falls back to the standard ~fill-paragraph~.

There is also ~line-fill-buffer~, which will step through the buffer and fill all paragraphs.

~line-fill-buffer~ is tested on some LaTeX documents but not very thoroughly---please check its output.

** Example

Before:

#+begin_example
The capybara is the largest living rodent. It is native to South America. Capybaras are highly social animals.
#+end_example

After ~M-x line-fill~:

#+begin_example
The capybara is the largest living rodent.
It is native to South America.
Capybaras are highly social animals.
#+end_example

** Abbreviation handling

The package will not insert a line break after abbreviations such as ~e.g.~,
~i.e.~, ~n.b.~, ~c.f.~, or single-letter initials.  It also avoids breaking
after ~!~ or ~?~ that appear inside parentheses (e.g. "faster (or more!) than
expected").

The list of non-breaking abbreviations is controlled by the variable
~line-fill-non-separators~.
